David J. Malan

The man who inspired me and million others

David J. Malan (on stage) teaching CS50 at Harvard University

David J. Malan is Gordon McKay Professor of the Practice of Computer Science at Harvard University. He is best known for teaching Computer Science 50 (CS50). CS50 is the largest course at both Harvard and Yale. The course is also available as MOOC at Edx and has been viewed by over a million people. Malan is very innovative and interactive teacher. He takes real world problems and connects them with programming, this is what makes him the best computer science professor.

Here are some facts about David J. Malan :

  • Malan studied government in freshman year at Harvard and ended up taking CS50 in the fall of 1996 as a sophomore which was taught by Brian Kernighan at that time.
  • Inspired by Kernighan he graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science in 1999. He completed Master of Science in 2004 followed by PhD in 2007 for research into cybersecurity and computer forensics.
  • He started teaching CS50 after completing his PhD and he is still doing so. In 2016 over 800 students enrolled in CS50 making it the largest course at both Harvard and Yale.
  • Along with CS50, he also teaches Computer Science 164, Computer Science E-1 etc. Theses courses can be found here.
  • All of his courses are freely available and licensed for re-use with attribution using OpenCourseWare, for example at cs50.tv and edx. Over a million people have viewed CS50 at Edx, making it one of the most popular courses there.
